Offline-First Architecture Tradeoffs

I explored adding offline support using WatermelonDB but ultimately decided to prioritize speed-to-market over a full offline-first architecture.

While offline features can be powerful, they introduce significant complexity‚Äîlocal and remote data sync, conflict resolution, and more state management. For an MVP, that tradeoff didn't make sense, especially given that most recipe app usage happens in connected environments like home or grocery stores with Wi-Fi. Only a small percentage of apps offer truly meaningful offline functionality.

The key takeaway was that thoughtful feature prioritization matters more than technical perfection. Shipping a focused, reliable product quickly often delivers more value than building complex features most users won't need.
